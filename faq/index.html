FAQ

The short version.

What is Lemma?

Lemma is a Bittensor subnet for machine-checkable mathematics. Miners produce Lean proofs, validators verify them mechanically, and rewards attach to proof correctness.

What is Lean?

Lean is a programming language and proof checker. It can read a formal proof and confirm whether each step follows from the rules.

What is formal mathematics?

Formal mathematics is math written in a precise language a computer can check. Definitions, statements, and proofs have exact meanings.

What is a theorem?

A theorem is a mathematical claim. In Lemma, the claim is written as a Lean statement so miners know exactly what must be proved.

What is a proof?

A proof is the chain of steps that establishes a theorem. A Lean proof is accepted only when Lean can check every step.

What are cadence tasks?

Cadence tasks are the automatic subnet work. Registered miners work on the current Lean theorem, often using a prover API, local tooling, or manual proof work to find a proof.

How long are cadence tasks?

They are block-based. Each theorem window is 100 blocks, about 20 minutes if blocks are about 12 seconds apart. The next theorem is chosen by the next fixed window, not by whether the last one was solved.

How does cadence scoring work?

Cadence scoring is automatic: validators check the submitted Lean file, and it passes or fails. Easy, medium, hard, and extreme tasks update difficulty-weighted rolling scores, and positive rolling scores become validator weights.

How are bounty rewards paid?

Bounties are separate manual campaigns with one listed reward for the accepted proof. They do not control automatic cadence validator weights.

What are bounty tasks?

Bounties are open campaigns for one selected theorem at a time. The dashboard shows the current bounty, reward, status, and source.

Do I have to be a miner to work on a bounty?

No. You do not need a subnet UID. Bounty submissions use a signed hotkey identity, and the first accepted proof wins the listed reward.

How are bounties paid?

Bounties are manual winner-take-all campaigns funded by the operator. The listed reward text is the public promise for that campaign.

What is OpenAI-compatible?

Cadence miners point lemma mine at an API that follows the OpenAI-style URL, API key, and model-id pattern. Manually prepared Lean proofs remain available through the advanced --submission path.

Do validators score explanations?

No. The live reward-critical artifact is the Lean proof. It passes or fails.

Why not show proof hashes or commitments on the site?

Public pages show task state and full solver hotkeys. Proof bodies, proof hashes, nonces, and commitment hashes stay out of the public website feed.

Where do bounty tasks come from?

Bounty tasks directly link their source. Current source material comes from Google DeepMind Formal Conjectures. Lemma is not affiliated with Google DeepMind. Formal Conjectures publishes software under Apache 2.0 and other materials under CC-BY 4.0, and Lemma pins the exact Lean declaration before a campaign opens.
