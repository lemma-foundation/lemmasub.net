FAQ

The short version.

What is Lemma?

Lemma is a Bittensor subnet for machine-checkable mathematics. Miners produce Lean proofs, validators verify them mechanically, and rewards attach to proof correctness.

What is Lean?

Lean is a programming language and proof checker. It can read a formal proof and confirm whether each step follows from the rules.

What is formal mathematics?

Formal mathematics is math written in a precise language a computer can check. Definitions, statements, and proofs have exact meanings.

What is a theorem?

A theorem is a mathematical claim. In Lemma, the claim is written as a Lean statement so miners know exactly what must be proved.

What is a proof?

A proof is the chain of steps that establishes a theorem. A Lean proof is accepted only when Lean can check every step.

What are cadence tasks?

Cadence tasks are recurring theorem targets for registered miners. They appear on the dashboard and feed automatic subnet rewards.

What are bounty tasks?

Bounties are focused campaigns for one selected theorem at a time. The current bounty also appears on the dashboard.

Do bounties require a UID?

No. They use a full hotkey signature for identity, not subnet registration.

How are cadence rewards paid?

Verified current-epoch cadence work earns (1 - solve_fraction)^2 of the budget, split by commitment rank. Unearned budget routes to the owner/burn UID.

How are bounties paid?

Bounties are manual winner-take-all campaigns funded from owner emission. The listed reward text is the public promise for that campaign.

What is OpenAI-compatible?

Optional prover assistance can point at an API that follows the OpenAI-style URL, API key, and model-id pattern. It is not required for manually prepared Lean proofs.

Do validators score explanations?

No. The live reward-critical artifact is the Lean proof. It passes or fails.

Why not show proof hashes or commitments on the site?

Public pages show task state and full solver hotkeys. Proof bodies, proof hashes, nonces, and commitment hashes stay out of the public website feed.

Where do bounty tasks come from?

Bounty tasks link to Google DeepMind Formal Conjectures sources and pin the exact Lean declaration before a campaign opens.
