How it works

A closed loop for formal proof work.

Lemma turns exact Lean statements into executable proof challenges. The verifier does not care how a proof was found. It checks whether the submitted proof passes the published target and policy, then the solved work can become a public artifact.

01

Source

Formal Conjectures supplies a pinned Lean statement.

02

Target

Lemma fixes the theorem, imports, toolchain, policy, and hash.

03

Prove

Miners search for a Submission.lean proof.

04

Verify

Validators run the pinned Lean and mathlib checker.

05

The subnet records that the exact target passed.

06

Publish

Lemma writes a proof artifact and prepares an upstream PR candidate.

Upstream loop

Every solved target should leave a public record.

Lemma's job does not end when a proof verifies. A solved target becomes a canonical artifact with source metadata, the accepted Lean submission, verifier output, and attestation details.

For Formal Conjectures targets, Lemma can prepare a pull request that marks the statement solved and links to the artifact through the upstream formal_proof mechanism.

Upstream review remains independent. Lemma verifies the exact formal target; upstream maintainers decide whether and how to accept the contribution.

Proof artifacts

The artifact binds target, proof, verifier, and provenance.

A proof artifact is the durable public record of a solved Lemma target. It lets anyone inspect what was proved, which statement was used, how it was checked, and how it can be cited upstream.

What gets published

  • source commit, file, and declaration
  • target hash and toolchain
  • accepted Submission.lean
  • verifier result and policy
  • attestation metadata
  • upstream PR status

Guardrails

Precise claims. Mechanical checks. Independent review.

Verification

Lean checks the exact formal target.

A proof passes or fails under the published theorem, imports, toolchain, target hash, and policy.

Review

Upstream acceptance is not automatic.

Formal Conjectures maintainers retain normal review authority over their repository.

Settlement

Upstream PRs are not the payout oracle.

Reward eligibility depends on Lemma's target, verifier, attestation, and subnet rules.

Participants

Four roles in the public proof loop.

Miners search.

They use models, tactics, retrieval, or human insight to produce Submission.lean.

Validators verify.

They run the pinned Lean checker and score verified proofs, not informal explanations.

Curators select.

They pin source metadata, toolchains, hashes, and target policies before work goes live.

Operators publish.

They review artifacts and upstream PR packages before public publication or contribution.
