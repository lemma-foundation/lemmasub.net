Machine-verified mathematics

Machine-verified mathematics, grown proof by proof.

Lemma is a permissionless incentive network for expanding open formal mathematics. Miners submit Lean proofs, validators verify them, and accepted proofs become replayable theorem/proof records in a public, citation-structured mathematical corpus.

What it is

An incentive layer for formal math.

Mathlib showed that machine-verified mathematics can become shared public infrastructure. Lemma adds an open incentive layer around that process: contributors compete to produce Lean proofs, validators check them, and accepted proof records become reusable mathematical building blocks.

The simple rule is: if the proof passes in the pinned Lean environment, it can become part of the corpus. If it fails, it earns nothing.

How it works

Four steps, one verifier.

Miners can use any method they want to search for proofs. Validators only care whether the final Lean proof passes the pinned verifier.

  1. 1 A theorem enters the active pool

    It gives the exact Lean statement to prove.

  2. 2 A miner submits a Lean proof

    The submission is bound to that theorem.

  3. 3 Validators verify the proof

    Lean gives a clean pass or fail.

  4. 4 The accepted proof is saved

    It becomes a replayable theorem/proof record in the open mathematical corpus.

Why math

Formal math has a clean reward signal.

A Lean proof either verifies or it does not. That gives Lemma a simple commodity: correct, machine-checkable mathematical work.

Math is also deep enough to stand on its own. Algebra, analysis, number theory, topology, probability, logic, and computer science already give the network years of useful work before it needs to broaden.

Downstream users can train theorem provers and reasoning models on the accepted corpus, but the core mission is simpler: grow open formal mathematics.

Tiny example

A proof can be short. Lean still decides.

This is a small example. Real tasks are harder, but the rule is the same: the Lean kernel accepts the proof or it does not.

Statement theorem add_zero_example (n : Nat) : n + 0 = n := by
Proof exact Nat.add_zero n
Pass

Lean accepts it, so the theorem/proof record can be kept.
