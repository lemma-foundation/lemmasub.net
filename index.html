How it works

Miners search. Validators verify. Lean arbitrates.

Lemma turns exact formal statements into executable proof challenges. The verifier does not care how a proof was found. It checks whether the submitted proof passes the published target and policy.

In Bittensor terms, miners produce work and validators measure it. In Lemma, the work is a Lean proof and the measurement is deterministic verification.

Formal Conjectures focus

From public conjectures to executable targets.

Lemma is being centered on Formal Conjectures, a public Lean 4 and mathlib repository of formalized mathematical statements. Lemma pins selected statements, publishes them as targets, and invites proof search under a reproducible checker.

Lemma is independent and is not endorsed by Google DeepMind or the Formal Conjectures authors.

Why formal verification

Plausible is not enough.

Many AI evaluations ask whether an answer sounds reasonable. Lemma asks whether a proof checks against an exact formal statement. The live correctness signal is mechanical.

Participants

Two jobs, one standard.

Miners search.

  • Pick a target from the registry.
  • Generate or write Submission.lean.
  • Verify locally before publishing an artifact.
  • Build payout packages only for live reward-backed targets.

Validators verify.

  • Fetch the exact target and registry hash.
  • Run the pinned Lean verifier.
  • Score verified proofs, not explanations.
  • Keep reward plumbing separate from correctness.
