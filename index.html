The problem

AI can generate reasoning traces. Training data needs reliable checks.

Fluent output is not enough for high-value reasoning data. Lemma focuses on artifacts that can be replayed, checked, and accepted or rejected by a verifier.

The result is a cleaner source of training signal: checked artifacts, open metadata, and a public corpus that can be reused outside the network.

How Lemma works

A simple miner, validator, corpus loop.

  1. Tasks are exposed Validators publish active verifier-checkable work for the network.
  2. Miners search Miners use any stack they want to produce candidate artifacts.
  3. Validators check The verifier decides whether an artifact passes in the pinned environment.
  4. The corpus grows Accepted artifacts become open training data for reasoning models.

Open corpus

Checked artifacts are the product.

Lemma is not a generic proof leaderboard. The useful output is reusable verifier-grounded training data: open, replayable, and tied to the verifier that accepted it.

Built for expansion

Lean is the first production domain, not the final boundary.

Lean theorem proving gives Lemma a mature deterministic verifier today. A submitted proof either passes in the pinned Lean environment or it does not.

The same network pattern can extend to other verifier domains over time: Rust verification, SAT and SMT certificates, optimization certificates, or other checked artifacts. Those are future possibilities, not launch promises.
