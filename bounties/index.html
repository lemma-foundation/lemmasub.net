Trustless bounties

No escrow, no live bounty.

Lemma bounties require funded Bittensor EVM escrow on Subtensor. A target can be interesting, drafted, and locally verifiable, but it is not an active reward offer until the escrow contract holds the funds.

The reward path is mechanical: commit on-chain, reveal on-chain, validator attestations after Lean verification, then contract release after the challenge window. No owner can redirect funds or manually bypass the release rules.

Note: Lemma is not affiliated with or partnered with Google DeepMind.

Claim process

Escrow first, proof second, payout by rule.

Cadence rounds are routine: miners receive theorem variants and validators check proof files. Bounties may involve longer proofs, helper lemmas, supporting files, and upstream review, so custody has to be settled before solvers spend time on them.

Fund a locked target

A live record includes the source URL, pinned repository commit or tag, Lean and Mathlib versions, target statement hash, policy version, toolchain id, escrow contract address, chain id, and escrow bounty id.

Commit and reveal

The first valid on-chain commitment can win. The solver reveals the proof artifact hash, salt, payout address, and hotkey binding before the reveal deadline.

Attest and release

Validators verify the revealed artifact with Lean, submit attestations, and wait through the challenge window. The contract releases payout only when the threshold is met.

Claim record

The payout address must be tied to the claimant.

Bittensor SS58 hotkeys and EVM H160 accounts use different key systems. A claim must bind the miner hotkey to the EVM claimant and payout address with an explicit signed message.

Review gates

High-value bounties need reproducible public evidence.

Before attestation, validators verify the proof in the pinned environment, match the exact target hash, reject shortcuts such as sorry, admit, new axioms, unsafe hooks, or unauthorized imports, and confirm the signed claimant binding.

The contract enforces custody, ordering, attestation threshold, challenge-window finality, and payout. Lean verification happens off-chain, but no validator or operator should handle reward custody.

Normal proof bounties should exclude targets that already had a Formal Conjectures formal_proof at bounty creation. Those targets can only be offered as explicit proof-porting or simplification work.
